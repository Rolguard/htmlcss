Recommendations

Pokemon Mystery Dungeon Explorers of Time

While I played this game on the Nintendo DS a long time ago, I revisited it in recent years using DeSmuME emulator playing a more DLC version called PokÃ©mon Mystery Dungeon (PMD): Explorers of Sky but overall they're the same game.

The action in the game is turn-based which can immediately be a turn-off for some but PMD breaks some of the monotonous grinding with large variety in regular and boss monsters and miscellaneous events such as Monster House and Kelecon's Shop.

Despite the combat being quite engaging, I think the main strength of this game is in the diversity of personalities, character development and engaging storytelling. But I won't be the first to admit that nostalgia from childhood may have severely clouded my judgement. At least, if you're a fan of RPGs, PMD will definitely be an enjoyable play.


Genre: Adventure, Story-based, Roguelike

Stardew Valley

Firstly, I want to lead off that the initial start of Stardey Valley was made by ONE single developer.

Eric "ConcernedApe" Barone programmed all the farming mechanics, designed all the character models whilst simultaneously integrating already-polished parts into a seamless and warm storyline of escaping the dullness of city life.

Stardew Valley presents colourful but also realistic characters each with their own motivations and struggles. What separates Stardew Valley from a game like PMD, is that players connect on a greater level as many empathise with the dread of office work and wish for a refreshing escape, particularly within nature. While I think that this game is best played solo (at least for your 1st playthrough), multiplayer options are available, building possibilities to build connections with the villagers, fish, mine and farm with your friends too.

Genre: Life/farming simulation, RPG, Adventure

Slay the Spire

This one is a definite banger and I know the deck-building / card-game genre will immediately push people off of this game but trust me... give it a go. I played the majority of my hours with only the three characters, Ironclad, Silent, Defect but they fairly recently released Watcher character which I tried a bit and looks pretty broken.

With plenty of "question mark" events, various elite and end level bosses each with their own special mechanics make learning this game extremely rewarding. Many bosses encourage completely different playstyles so even if you use the same deck, gameplay is exceptionally diverse. On top of various builds for characters like strength/block-stacking on Ironclad, shiv and poison builds on Silent and orb mechanics with Defect, replayability is further reinforced, passing Slay the Spire as one of my favourite roguelite's of all time.

Genre: Deck-building, Strategy, Roguelike
